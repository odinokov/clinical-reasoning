# Branch D — Laboratory, Imaging, and Pathology Interpretation

Use this reference when the query concerns a lab value, imaging finding, pathology result, or biomarker.

---

## # Interpretation

Cover the following elements in order:

### Classification
Normal / abnormal / critical / indeterminate. Include the reference range and mark if the range is age-, sex-, population-, or assay-specific.

### Pathophysiologic significance
What the finding reflects biologically. Connect the number or image to the underlying physiology or anatomy.

### Leading etiologic categories
Organize by mechanism (not just an alphabetical list). Examples:
- For elevated ferritin: acute-phase reactant vs iron overload vs hepatic/muscle injury vs malignancy vs macrophage activation
- For elevated troponin: ischemic (type 1 MI, type 2 supply-demand) vs non-ischemic (myocarditis, PE, sepsis, CKD, HF)
- For unilateral pulmonary infiltrate: infection vs aspiration vs infarction vs neoplasm vs inflammation

### Relevant syndrome correlations
Link the finding to defined syndromes when applicable (e.g., anion gap metabolic acidosis → GOLD MARK; microcytic anemia → TAILS; macrocytic anemia with MCV pattern).

## Preanalytic pitfalls

These errors occur before the sample reaches the analyzer. Always consider:

- **Sample type and tube additive:** serum vs plasma, EDTA vs citrate vs heparin, transport media
- **Timing:** fasting vs non-fasting, time of day for diurnal hormones (cortisol, testosterone, ACTH, TSH), days since symptom onset for serologies
- **Posture:** supine vs seated vs standing for renin, aldosterone, cortisol, catecholamines
- **Tourniquet time:** prolonged application elevates potassium, lactate, hemoconcentrates
- **Hemolysis:** elevates K, LDH, AST, phosphate, magnesium, Fe; lowers sodium (spuriously)
- **Lipemia:** spurious hyponatremia via displacement (with older indirect ISE methods), interferes with bilirubin, LDH
- **Icterus:** interferes with bilirubin (spuriously), some enzymes
- **Medication interference:** biotin (see below), ascorbic acid (falsely negative occult blood), levodopa, etc.
- **Contamination:** IV line draws giving spurious glucose, K, etc.

## Analytic pitfalls

These errors occur during measurement on the analyzer:

### Assay platform differences
Results can vary significantly by platform. Examples:
- **Troponin:** high-sensitivity vs contemporary assays have different 99th percentile URLs and different serial change thresholds
- **25-OH vitamin D:** immunoassay vs LC-MS/MS — immunoassays may not cross-react fully with D2 vs D3
- **Testosterone:** immunoassay unreliable at low levels (women, children, hypogonadal men); use LC-MS/MS
- **Estradiol:** immunoassay vs LC-MS/MS similar issue
- **TSH:** third-generation assay required for suppressed TSH quantification
- **Cortisol:** immunoassay cross-reacts with prednisolone and other steroids; LC-MS/MS more specific
- **HbA1c:** affected by hemoglobinopathies, high red cell turnover, uremia; gold standard IFCC method

### Heterophile antibody interference
Particularly troponin, thyroid function tests, HCG. Falsely elevated or suppressed; suspect when result is discordant with clinical picture. Confirm by serial dilution, alternative assay, or blocker.

### Biotin interference
Patients on high-dose biotin supplementation (≥5 mg/day, common in supplements marketed for hair/skin/nails, MS) can produce falsely **low** troponin and TSH, falsely **high** free T4, free T3. Hold biotin ≥8 hours before testing where possible.

### Hook effect (high-dose hook)
Extremely elevated antigen saturates immunoassay, producing falsely **low** or **normal** result. Suspect with beta-HCG in molar pregnancy, choriocarcinoma; ferritin in hemophagocytic syndromes; some tumor markers. Dilution resolves.

### Cross-reactivity
- Immunoassays for drugs cross-react with structurally similar compounds (amphetamine screens positive with pseudoephedrine, bupropion, selegiline; opiate screens miss synthetic opioids)
- Always confirm screening drug-of-abuse positives with definitive testing (GC-MS, LC-MS/MS)

### Carryover
Rare but possible — very high sample contaminating subsequent sample on automated analyzer.

## Postanalytic pitfalls

These errors occur in interpretation after the result is reported:

### Inappropriate reference range
- Range derived from population that doesn't match patient (adult range for pediatric; non-pregnant for pregnant; reference range not adjusted for race/ethnicity where relevant)
- Race-based adjustment for eGFR has been removed from CKD-EPI (2021 equation)
- Creatinine reference range assumes normal muscle mass
- Reference range not adjusted for altitude (e.g., hematocrit)

### Non-gaussian distribution
Reference ranges from 2.5–97.5 percentile assume gaussian distribution; many labs aren't gaussian (e.g., CK, ferritin). "Just above normal" may not be meaningful.

### Diurnal / cyclic variation
- Cortisol: peaks AM, nadir late evening
- TSH: peaks at night
- Testosterone: peaks AM
- Prolactin: peaks in sleep, after meals, after exercise, after breast stimulation
- Iron: diurnal variation; fasting AM preferred
- Menstrual cycle affects FSH, LH, estradiol, progesterone interpretation

### Trend vs single value
A single abnormal value often matters less than a trend. Always ask:
- Is this a new change or chronic?
- What's the rate of change?
- Does it correlate with clinical status?

## False-positive and false-negative considerations

State estimated rates where known. For example:
- D-dimer: high sensitivity, poor specificity; false positives common (inflammation, malignancy, pregnancy, age)
- Troponin: rises in many non-ACS conditions; dynamic change more informative than single value
- BNP/NT-proBNP: elevated in CKD, age, female sex; suppressed in obesity
- Fecal occult blood: affected by diet, NSAIDs, vitamin C
- Autoimmune serologies: low positive predictive value in unselected populations

## Value of repeat, confirmatory, or trend testing

Name when each is indicated:
- **Repeat** same test: suspected lab error, transient physiologic variation, confirmation of critical result
- **Confirmatory** (different test): drug screen positive → GC-MS; elevated aldosterone/renin ratio → salt loading test; positive HIV screen → confirmatory antibody/antigen differentiation and RNA
- **Trend / kinetics:** troponin 0/1h or 0/3h, HCG doubling in pregnancy, CRP trajectory, LFT trajectory

---

## Clinical correlation reminder

Before acting on any result:
- Does the result fit the clinical picture?
- If not, consider preanalytic error, analytic interference, or missed alternative diagnosis — in that order.
- Apply pretest probability (see Bayesian framework): a positive test in a low-pretest-probability patient is more likely a false positive than a true positive.
