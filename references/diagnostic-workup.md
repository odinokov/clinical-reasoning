# Branch A — Diagnostic Workup

Use this reference when the query concerns symptoms, syndromes, differential diagnosis, or workup strategy.

---

## # Differential diagnosis

Rank diagnoses from highest to lowest posterior probability. Use a structured etiologic framework where the presentation warrants one:
- **VITAMIN CDE** (Vascular, Infectious, Traumatic, Autoimmune, Metabolic, Iatrogenic, Neoplastic, Congenital, Degenerative, Endocrine)
- **VINDICATE** (Vascular, Infectious, Neoplastic, Degenerative, Idiopathic/Iatrogenic, Congenital, Autoimmune, Traumatic, Endocrine)
- Anatomic (by structure from outside in or proximal to distal)
- System-based
- Mechanism-based (inflammation, ischemia, obstruction, toxicity, genetic)

For each diagnosis:

- **Probability tier:** high / intermediate / low, with qualitative pretest probability anchor
- **Why it fits:** clinical features, epidemiology, mechanism
- **Why it may not fit:** inconsistent features, atypical demographics
- **Key discriminating features:** specific findings on history, exam, or testing
- **Recommended confirmatory or exclusionary workup:** with expected LR+ / LR− when known
- **Consequence if missed:** morbidity, mortality, reversibility window
- **Urgent rule-out required?** yes / no
- **Applicable validated clinical decision rule or score**, if one exists (e.g., Wells, PERC, HEART, Centor, Alvarado, Ottawa Ankle/Knee, NEXUS, PECARN, ROSIER)

## # Recommended diagnostic workup

Organize by modality:

### History
Include relevant elements:
- **OPQRST** (Onset, Provocation/Palliation, Quality, Radiation, Severity, Timing) for pain
- **OLDCARTS** (Onset, Location, Duration, Character, Aggravating, Relieving, Timing, Severity)
- **HEADSS** (Home, Education, Activities, Drugs, Sex/Sexuality, Suicide/Depression) for adolescents
- **SAMPLE** (Signs/Symptoms, Allergies, Medications, Past history, Last oral intake, Events) for emergency triage
- Exposure history: travel, occupation, hobbies, pets, sick contacts
- Family history with age of onset
- Medication reconciliation (prescription, OTC, supplements, recreational)
- Sexual, reproductive, and substance use histories when relevant

### Physical examination
- Specific maneuvers with known LR (e.g., Murphy sign, McBurney point, Lasegue, Spurling, Tinel, Finkelstein, Hawkins)
- Vital sign patterns (shock index, pulse pressure, orthostatic change, respiratory pattern)
- Focused system exam indicated by the problem representation
- Functional assessment (gait, strength, range of motion) when relevant

### Laboratory testing
Organize as:
- **Basic:** CBC with differential, BMP/CMP, UA, pregnancy test, glucose, coagulation panel
- **Targeted:** troponin, BNP/NT-proBNP, lipase, LFTs, TSH, lactate, CRP, procalcitonin, D-dimer, ABG/VBG, blood cultures
- **Specialized:** autoimmune panels, specific antibodies, hormone panels, tumor markers, heavy metals, drug levels
- Specify the **assay platform** when it changes interpretation (immunoassay vs mass spectrometry vs enzymatic)

### Imaging
Specify:
- Modality (XR, US, CT, MRI, PET, nuclear medicine, fluoroscopy)
- With or without contrast (and contrast type: iodinated vs gadolinium)
- Specific protocol (e.g., CT PE protocol, MRI liver with eovist, CT renal stone without contrast)
- Expected findings supporting each differential
- Radiation / contrast risk considerations

### Microbiology / pathology / molecular diagnostics
- Cultures (blood, urine, CSF, sputum, stool, wound) with timing relative to antibiotics
- Gram stain, AFB, fungal stain
- PCR and NAAT (specify organism panel)
- Sequencing (16S, metagenomic, NGS oncology panel)
- Biopsy: incisional vs excisional vs core vs fine-needle
- Cytology, immunohistochemistry, flow cytometry, FISH, cytogenetics

### Functional / physiologic testing
- EKG (12-lead, rhythm strip, continuous monitoring, event recorder, Holter)
- Echocardiography (TTE, TEE, stress echo)
- Pulmonary function testing (spirometry, DLCO, body plethysmography)
- EEG, EMG, nerve conduction studies
- Sleep study (polysomnography, home sleep apnea test)
- Cardiac stress testing (exercise, pharmacologic, imaging-based)
- Urodynamic, manometric, motility studies

### Specialist referral
- Specify timing (emergent, urgent, routine)
- Specify the clinical question for the consultant
- Note whether inpatient vs outpatient

### For each test
- **Purpose:** what clinical question it answers
- **Expected LR+ / LR− or sensitivity/specificity** when known
- **What result would shift the differential** and how
- **Critical value or threshold** mandating action
- **Urgency:** urgent, routine, or conditional on prior result
- **Preanalytic, analytic, or postanalytic pitfalls** specific to this test

## Common "can't-miss" differentials by presentation

These are the default urgent rule-outs that should appear early in differentials when the presentation is compatible:

- **Chest pain:** ACS, PE, aortic dissection, tension pneumothorax, esophageal rupture, pericarditis with tamponade
- **Severe headache:** SAH, meningitis/encephalitis, temporal arteritis, cerebral venous thrombosis, hypertensive emergency, carbon monoxide
- **Acute abdomen:** mesenteric ischemia, ruptured AAA, perforated viscus, ectopic pregnancy, ovarian/testicular torsion, incarcerated hernia, necrotizing pancreatitis
- **Syncope:** arrhythmia, PE, aortic stenosis, SAH, AAA, ectopic pregnancy, GI bleed
- **Acute back pain:** cauda equina, spinal epidural abscess, aortic dissection, vertebral fracture, malignancy
- **Altered mental status:** hypoglycemia, hypoxia, hypercarbia, opioid/benzodiazepine overdose, CNS infection, stroke, NCSE, Wernicke encephalopathy, thyroid storm, adrenal crisis
- **Dyspnea:** PE, ACS, tension pneumothorax, tamponade, anaphylaxis, upper airway obstruction
- **Shock:** hemorrhagic, cardiogenic, obstructive (PE, tamponade, tension pneumothorax), distributive (septic, anaphylactic, neurogenic)
- **Pediatric fever without source:** meningitis, UTI, occult bacteremia, Kawasaki, appendicitis, intussusception, testicular torsion
- **Pregnancy with pain/bleeding:** ectopic, placental abruption, previa, preeclampsia with severe features, HELLP, uterine rupture

## Bayesian discipline reminder

Before finalizing the workup plan:
- Is pretest probability high enough to justify testing, or is the test more likely to generate a false positive than inform management?
- Is there a test result that would NOT change management? If so, don't order it.
- Is confirmatory testing needed after a positive result, and if so, name it.
