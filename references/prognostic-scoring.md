# Branch C — Prognostic Scoring and Risk Stratification

Use this reference when the query concerns outcome, trajectory, or risk stratification.

---

## # Prognosis

Cover the following elements:

### Expected clinical trajectory
- Natural history **without treatment**
- Natural history **with standard treatment**
- Typical inflection points (acute phase → recovery, stable → progressive)

### Short-term risks
- Inpatient mortality
- 30-day mortality or readmission
- Early complications

### Long-term outcomes
- 1-year, 5-year, lifetime estimates where meaningful
- Progression-free interval, relapse risk, recurrence rate

### Complication profile
Organized by system and by timing (early, intermediate, late).

### Prognostic modifiers
- Demographics (age, sex, race/ethnicity effects on natural history)
- Comorbidities that worsen prognosis
- Biomarkers (e.g., troponin, BNP, lactate, procalcitonin, albumin, INR, LDH)
- Imaging findings (e.g., ejection fraction, tumor burden, infarct volume)
- Response-to-therapy indicators (early responders vs non-responders)
- Functional status at presentation

### Markers of deterioration or recovery
Specific clinical, lab, or imaging parameters to trend.

### Impact on patient-important outcomes
- Mortality
- Functional status
- Quality of life
- Hospitalization
- Return to work/baseline activities

Avoid relying on surrogate markers alone for prognostic framing.

## Validated prognostic scores

Use named scores where applicable. Include score name, what it predicts, and the threshold that changes management.

### Cardiology
- **CHA₂DS₂-VASc** — stroke risk in AF; score ≥2 (men) or ≥3 (women) generally supports anticoagulation
- **HAS-BLED** — bleeding risk on anticoagulation
- **ATRIA** / **ORBIT** — alternative AF stroke/bleed scores
- **GRACE** — in-hospital and 6-month mortality post-ACS
- **TIMI risk score** — ACS, STEMI, UA/NSTEMI variants
- **HEART score** — ED chest pain 6-week MACE risk
- **Framingham Risk Score** / **ASCVD Pooled Cohort Equations** — 10-year CV risk
- **Seattle Heart Failure Model**, **MAGGIC** — HF prognosis
- **EuroSCORE II**, **STS** — cardiac surgical risk
- **SCAI SHOCK** — cardiogenic shock classification

### Pulmonary / critical care
- **Wells score** (PE and DVT variants) — pretest probability for PE/DVT
- **PERC rule** — rule-out criteria in very-low-risk PE
- **Geneva score** — alternative PE pretest probability
- **PESI / sPESI** — 30-day mortality in acute PE
- **CURB-65** / **Pneumonia Severity Index (PSI)** — community-acquired pneumonia severity
- **SMART-COP** — CAP ICU admission prediction
- **SOFA / qSOFA** — sepsis organ dysfunction and rapid screen
- **APACHE II/III/IV** — ICU mortality
- **SAPS II/III** — ICU severity
- **GCS** — consciousness level; ≤8 generally triggers intubation consideration

### Neurology
- **NIHSS** — stroke severity at presentation
- **mRS** — disability after stroke (0–6, 6 = death)
- **ABCD²** — post-TIA stroke risk
- **Hunt-Hess**, **WFNS**, **Fisher** — SAH grading
- **CRASH / IMPACT** — TBI outcome

### Gastroenterology / hepatology
- **MELD / MELD-Na / MELD 3.0** — cirrhosis mortality and transplant priority
- **Child-Pugh** — cirrhosis prognosis and drug dosing reference
- **Maddrey Discriminant Function** — alcoholic hepatitis, DF ≥32 supports steroids
- **Lille score** — alcoholic hepatitis steroid response at day 7
- **Glasgow-Blatchford** / **AIMS65** / **Rockall** — UGI bleed risk
- **Oakland score** — LGI bleed outpatient management
- **Ranson** / **BISAP** / **APACHE II** — acute pancreatitis severity
- **Truelove-Witts** — severe UC

### Oncology / hematology
- **ECOG / Karnofsky** — performance status
- **IPI / R-IPI / FLIPI / MIPI** — lymphoma prognostic indices
- **IPSS / IPSS-R / IPSS-M** — MDS prognosis
- **ISS / R-ISS / R2-ISS** — multiple myeloma staging
- **TNM** — solid tumor staging (edition matters)
- **Gleason + Grade Group** — prostate cancer
- **BCLC** — hepatocellular carcinoma staging and treatment allocation
- **Charlson Comorbidity Index** — long-term mortality adjustment
- **Oncotype DX / MammaPrint** — breast cancer recurrence genomic scoring

### Infectious disease
- **CURB-65**, **PSI** (see pulmonary)
- **SOFA / qSOFA** (see critical care)
- **Pitt bacteremia score** — bacteremia mortality
- **NEWS2 / MEWS** — general ward deterioration warning

### Renal
- **KDIGO AKI staging** — acute kidney injury severity
- **AKIN** / **RIFLE** — legacy AKI staging
- **CKD staging (G1–G5, A1–A3)** — using CKD-EPI eGFR and albuminuria
- **Kidney Failure Risk Equation (KFRE)** — 2- and 5-year risk of ESRD

### Obstetrics / perinatal
- **Bishop score** — cervical ripening / readiness for induction
- **Apgar** — 1- and 5-minute neonatal assessment
- **sFlt-1/PlGF ratio** — preeclampsia prediction
- **fullPIERS** — adverse maternal outcome in preeclampsia

### Trauma
- **ISS** — injury severity score
- **RTS** — revised trauma score
- **TRISS** — survival probability combining RTS + ISS

### Frailty / geriatrics
- **Clinical Frailty Scale** (1–9)
- **Fried Frailty Phenotype**
- **Rockwood Frailty Index**

### Mental health
- **PHQ-9** — depression severity
- **GAD-7** — anxiety severity
- **C-SSRS** — suicide risk screening
- **AUDIT / CIWA** — alcohol use and withdrawal
- **HAM-D**, **MADRS** — clinician-administered depression scales

## How to present scores in output

When a score is relevant:
- Name the score
- State what it predicts
- Give the patient's calculated value only if inputs are confirmed (otherwise state inputs needed)
- Name the actionable threshold
- Note the score's limitations (e.g., derivation population, external validity)

Example: "CHA₂DS₂-VASc for stroke risk in AF. Score 3 in this patient (age ≥75, HTN, female sex). At score ≥2 in men / ≥3 in women, anticoagulation is generally indicated absent bleeding contraindication. Score derivation was primarily in non-valvular AF; does not apply to valvular AF or AF ≤48h in select scenarios."

---

## Risk communication discipline

- Quantify only when underlying data are reliable
- If using a score outside its validated population, flag this
- Distinguish **absolute risk** from **relative risk**
- When communicating to non-clinicians: use natural frequencies ("3 out of 100") over percentages; pair benefit with harm
